üéØ Limits & Continuity - Getting Close to the Truth

üèÉ‚Äç‚ôÇÔ∏èWhat are Limits?

Imagine you're walking toward a treasure chest. The limit is where the treasure should be, even if you can't quite reach it!

üìö Limit Definition

We say f(x,y) approaches L as (x,y) approaches (a,b) if:

This means: as we get closer to point (a,b), f(x,y) gets closer to L

‚ö†Ô∏è Tricky Part!

Unlike single variables, we can approach a point from infinitely many directions!

The limit must be the same from ALL directions, or it doesn't exist.

üåü Simple Example

Find: lim(x,y)‚Üí(1,2) (3x + 2y)

Just substitute! (This works for continuous functions)
= 3(1) + 2(2) = 3 + 4 = 7

üí° Pro Tips for Limits

  • Try direct substitution first
  • If you get 0/0, try different approaches (polar coordinates, squeeze theorem)
  • Check multiple paths - if different paths give different limits, the limit doesn't exist

üîÑ Continuity

A function is continuous at (a,b) if:

f(a,b) exists (function is defined there)
lim(x,y)‚Üí(a,b) f(x,y) exists
lim(x,y)‚Üí(a,b) f(x,y) = f(a,b)

Translation: No jumps, holes, or breaks in the surface!
